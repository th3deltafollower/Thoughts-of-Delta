	When an individual tramples apon the will of a person, they create a debt that they now owe to that person. This debt isn't monetary, but is based on
the concept of 'karma'. If the person gives a favor equivalent to damage caused, or, if the person takes injury equivalent to damage caused, then the debt is
considered paid. Interest is not a factor in this debt, but is replaced with something else entirely: Resentment. If a spiritual debt goes unpaid for a long 
period of time, or if deliberately ignored, resentment towards the debtor is likely to grow within the person harmed. It's only natural for the latter of the
two payment methods to be used if too much time has passed, so it's better to either apologize and get it over with or simply not trample another's will in 
the first place. 

	There are some people who gather large amounts of debt, partially due to foolishness, partially due to them thinking themselves invincible. In such 
cases--particulary when the harm outweighs the cost of a human life--debt is subject to consolidation and the debtor is to be 'removed'. (1)
